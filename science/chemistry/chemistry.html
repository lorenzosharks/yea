About an atom

Ionization Energy
  • Energy it takes to remove an electron completely
  • Equal and opposite to Coulombic Potential Energy
  • Measured in kilojoules per molecules
  • Increases across a period and decreases across a group
  • Number of protons is its atomic number

  • Unless otherwise stated, number of electrons is equal to number of protons

  • An isotope of an atom is the same number of protons but a different number of electrons

  • Size is 10⁻¹⁰ meters

  • Cations are atoms that loses electrons have a positive charge (metals do this more often)

  • Anions are atoms that gains electrons to have a negative charge (nonmetals do this more often)

  • Ground state is the atom at its lowest energy (ie. # of protons is equal to # of electrons)

  • Absorption is when a photon only makes an electron jump to an outside shell

  • Emission is when an electron loses energy and falls down a shell

  • The electron affinity of an atom is the energy change resulting from adding an electron to a gaseous atom

  • The valence shell is the outermost electron shell

  • Potential energy increases as an electron gets farther away from the nucleus

  • Attractive force of the nucleus decreases as the electron gets farther away from the nucleus

Coulombic's Law of Electrostatics

  • \(q_{1}\) and \(q_{2}\) represents the masses of the atoms
  • \(k_{e}\) is a constant
  • \(r\) is the distance between the centers of the atoms
  • \(F_{12}\) and \(F_{21}\) is the force by \(q_{1}\) on \(q_{2}\) and \(q_{2}\) on \(q_{1}\) respectively

Atomic Bonds

  • Bonding is maximizing attractive forces and nonpolar covalent bonds repulsive forces

  • The attractive force here is the nucleus pulling on the other atom's electrons

  • The valence shell is what determines the bonding properties

  • If the outside shell is completely closed, it is considered to be very unreactive

  • Electronegativity (\(EN\)) is the tendency of an atom to attract electrons when participating in a bond

  • \(\Delta EN\) is the absolute value of electronegativity difference

  • Only nonmetals form covalent bonds

  • Only the "\(-\)" shows a covalent bond (ie. \(H-H\))

  • A molecule is when 2 or more atoms are joined together by covalent bonds

Nonpolar covalent bonds
  • \(\Delta EN \leq 0.7\)
  • An equal amount of attraction occurs on both atoms

Polar covalent bonds
  • \(\Delta EN > 0.7\) and \(\leq 2\)
  • The attraction from an atom on another atom is greater than its reciprocal

Ionic bonds
  • \(\Delta EN > 2\)
  • The attraction from an atom on another atom is greater than its reciprocal

Partial charges
  • It holds everything together
  • It is represented by \(\delta^{+}\) and \(\delta^{-}\)
  • The larger atom is labeled as \(\delta^{-}\)
  • The smaller atom is labeled as \(\delta^{+}\)

Periodic table

  • Number of elements in each row corresponds to how many electrons are in that shell

  • Going down one row adds one extra shell

  • Going across one column adds an electron to the valence shell

  • Both electron affinity and ionization energy increases from left to right and top to bottom

  • The period is each row and the group is each column
